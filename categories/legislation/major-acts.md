### Economic Policy

Alexander Hamilton's Financial Plan (1790) proposed that the federal government assume the war debts of the states, establishing national creditworthiness and binding the states more closely to the federal government. The plan was controversial — Southern states that had already paid their debts objected — but a deal was struck: assumption of debts in exchange for placing the national capital on the Potomac River. Congress chartered the First Bank of the United States in 1791 for a 20-year term, providing the nation with a central institution for managing government funds, issuing currency, and making loans. The Tariff of 1789 established the first revenue system for the new government and set the precedent for using tariffs to protect domestic manufacturing.

### Civil Rights

The Civil Rights Act of 1866 was the first federal law to define citizenship and affirm that all citizens were equally protected by the law. Passed over President Andrew Johnson's veto, it declared that all persons born in the United States (excluding Native Americans) were citizens and entitled to equal rights regardless of race. This act laid the groundwork for the 14th Amendment and established the principle of federal enforcement of civil rights against state action.
