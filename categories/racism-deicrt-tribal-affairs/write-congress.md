### SAMPLE LETTER

Dear Sir or Madam,

This is not solely about racism within this administration. It is about the systematic marginalization of entire groups of people, the normalization of hate-filled rhetoric that incites hostility and violence, and the elevation of individuals who embrace extremist ideologies.

Native American rights are being infringed upon. Black voters are being disenfranchised. Americans who are not white are increasingly subjected to racial profiling. Islamophobia and hostility toward Muslims are being openly promoted. Programs designed to help disadvantaged and historically marginalized communities are being dismantled.

At the same time, individuals who embrace ideologies such as the Great Replacement Theory and Manifest Destiny are being placed in positions of influence and authority. Public officials have faced little or no accountability for sharing blatantly racist memes. Groups of Republican lawmakers have been caught participating in or sharing content from deeply racist online forums. Senators have displayed Nazi symbolism in their offices without meaningful consequence.

Perhaps most alarming, white supremacists are being pardoned, appointed to positions of power, receiving financial support, permitted to establish 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations, or, in some cases, all of the above. These are not isolated incidents. They reflect a disturbing pattern of tolerating, empowering, and legitimizing extremist views that have no place in a constitutional republic.

How can you support a political movement that repeatedly enables and elevates white supremacist ideology? The following outlines my concerns in greater detail. Some examples are included to provide historical context and demonstrate how these patterns have developed over time.
