### SAMPLE LETTER

Dear Sir or Madam,

You cannot continue treating the LGBTQ+ community as though they are somehow less deserving of the rights, dignity, and protections afforded to every other citizen of this country. They are ordinary people living ordinary lives. They serve in our military, care for the sick in our hospitals, raise families, work in factories and assembly lines, feed the hungry, and contribute to virtually every aspect of American society. They are our coworkers, our friends, our neighbors, and our fellow citizens.

There is no justification for shaming, excluding, or endangering them by promoting rhetoric that fuels prejudice and hostility. No individual or institution has the right to impose an extreme religious ideology on the public or to determine who is worthy of equal treatment under the law. Citizenship and civil rights are not contingent upon conformity to any one religious belief.

The LGBTQ+ community is not composed of criminals, nor should its members be portrayed as threats to society. If one chooses to invoke religious principles, it is worth remembering that hatred, condemnation, and the judgment of one's neighbor stand in direct opposition to the values of compassion and justice that many faiths profess. The continued spread of propaganda that vilifies an entire group of people serves only to divide communities and encourage discrimination.

The following sections outline my concerns in greater detail. Some of the information is included to provide historical context and demonstrate the broader pattern that has led to the issues addressed in this letter.
