### Cabinet

Washington assembled the first presidential cabinet, appointing Thomas Jefferson as Secretary of State, Alexander Hamilton as Secretary of the Treasury, Henry Knox as Secretary of War, and Edmund Randolph as Attorney General. The ideological rivalry between Hamilton and Jefferson would shape American politics for decades.

### Foreign Policy

The Proclamation of Neutrality in 1793 kept the young nation out of the war between France and Britain. The controversial Jay Treaty of 1795 resolved lingering issues with Britain but drew fierce opposition from Democratic-Republicans.

### Domestic Policy

The Whiskey Rebellion of 1794 tested federal authority when western Pennsylvania farmers resisted the excise tax on whiskey. Washington led a militia force to suppress the uprising, establishing the precedent of federal enforcement. His Farewell Address in 1796 warned against political factions and foreign entanglements.
