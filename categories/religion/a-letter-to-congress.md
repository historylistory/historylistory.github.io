or

### SAMPLE LETTER

Dear Sir or Madam,

Freedom From Religion Foundation Co-President Annie Laurie Gaylor wrote, "Our Constitution is godless by design. The government has no authority to organize religious revivals, much less use them to promote a Christian nationalist agenda."

That is precisely the issue we are facing today. Government institutions are increasingly being used to advance a particular religious and political ideology instead of remaining neutral.

Students are having the Ten Commandments and biblical stories pushed on them in public schools, excluding other religions and nonreligious viewpoints. Outside of school, organizations such as PragerU and BentKey continue that indoctrination through media aimed at children. Schools already have debate teams and other extracurricular activities. The government has no business incorporating Turning Point USA propaganda into public education. If families want to participate in those organizations, they are free to do so privately. Taxpayers should not be funding political indoctrination or enriching another 501(c)(3) organization that leverages religion for influence and profit. We have seen how figures such as Erika Kirk profit by selling merchandise and soliciting donations. Why should the government be promoting that?

The Military Religious Freedom Foundation received hundreds of complaints from every branch of the military after commanders reportedly told subordinates that the war in Iran was a biblical mandate and that President Trump had been anointed by God to ignite Armageddon. That has no place in the United States military.

At the same time, a growing culture of personality has elevated Donald Trump to near-messianic status. Religious figures such as Paula White have compared him to Jesus, images portraying him as Jesus or the Pope are widely circulated, golden statues of Trump have become recurring symbols, and Trump has repeatedly shared or amplified this imagery on Truth Social rather than rejecting it. That is not Christianity—it is ego and idolatry.

Many Trump-aligned religious organizations also sympathize with white nationalist movements and display "Appeal to Heaven" signs outside their homes and workplaces. Historically, movements that exploit religion for political power have caused immense harm. These organizations weaponize faith for money, power, and influence. They are wolves in sheep's clothing, using religion to divide Americans instead of bringing them together.

No one is arguing against anyone's right to practice their religion. The objection is to using government power to promote one religious ideology over all others. That movement is performative, politically motivated, and transparently self-serving. It misuses faith to divide, conquer, and enrich itself, and that is why so many people reject it.
