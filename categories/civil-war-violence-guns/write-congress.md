### SAMPLE LETTER

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am deeply concerned by the growing normalization of division, hostility, and political tribalism in our public discourse. A healthy democracy depends on citizens being able to disagree without viewing one another as enemies. Yet too often, political rhetoric is driven by outrage, fear, and resentment rather than by facts, reason, and a commitment to the common good.

Leaders have a responsibility to reduce tensions and strengthen civic trust. When they instead benefit from amplifying conflict and encouraging citizens to distrust one another, our institutions, communities, and democracy suffer.

America is strongest when its leaders seek to unite rather than divide, to persuade rather than inflame, and to elevate public discourse rather than exploit our differences. I believe we are falling short of that standard, and the examples below illustrate why these concerns deserve serious attention.

Below are examples of which events highlight these concerns. Some of the points are there for historical context.
