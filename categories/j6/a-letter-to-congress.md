### SAMPLE LETTER

Dear Sir or Madam,

It is astonishing that you would allow a sitting president to remain in office after inciting an insurrection against the United States Capitol. That fact alone should have disqualified him from holding public office. Rather than condemning those responsible, he has consistently celebrated and defended them.

Trump referred to the January 6 insurrectionists as "hostages" and described the attack as a "beautiful day." He even attempted to establish a fund to provide them with financial support, despite the serious crimes for which many were convicted. The so-called "J6 Prison Choir" is not a symbol of patriotism—it is a disgrace. That Trump chose to participate in promoting its debut album only reinforces his willingness to glorify criminal conduct.

Many of the individuals involved in the January 6 attack were members of extremist organizations such as the Oath Keepers and the Proud Boys. Among those convicted or charged were repeat violent offenders, individuals convicted of sexually assaulting children, violent rapists, terrorists who planned violence against elected officials, and people responsible for violently assaulting—and in some cases contributing to the deaths of—law enforcement officers. Pardoning individuals from this group sends a dangerous message that political violence is excusable when it serves a preferred cause.

Inciting violence against our democratic institutions was already an extraordinary abuse of power. Pardoning those who carried out that violence only compounds it. Such actions undermine the rule of law, diminish accountability, and erode public trust in our institutions.

Trump's conduct in both encouraging the attack and subsequently celebrating and rewarding those responsible is indefensible.
