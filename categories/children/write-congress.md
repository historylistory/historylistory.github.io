If this topic matters to you, write your Senator or write Congress. Send them an email.

### SAMPLE LETTER

Dear Sir or Madam,

I find the current administration's treatment of children, both in the United States and abroad, morally indefensible. Time and again, this administration has supported, defended, or pursued policies that place political objectives above the welfare of children.

It has continued to support military actions that have resulted in the deaths of countless children. It has condoned family separation and harsh immigration policies that inflict lasting trauma on minors. It has failed to treat school shootings as the national emergency they are. It has sought to shape educational institutions around a narrow ideological agenda rather than fostering critical thinking and intellectual independence.

I am equally disturbed by actions that have threatened children's health and well-being. Reductions in support for food assistance programs have left vulnerable families struggling to feed their children. Cuts to pediatric research and public health initiatives jeopardize the lives of young people who depend on those programs. The normalization of anti-vaccine rhetoric risks undermining decades of medical progress and exposing children to preventable diseases. Reports of food aid being destroyed or withheld while children face hunger around the world are especially troubling.

The measure of a society is how it treats its most vulnerable members. Children cannot vote, hire lobbyists, or defend themselves in the political arena. They depend on adults and institutions to protect them. When a government repeatedly adopts policies that harm children, neglect their needs, or treat their welfare as an afterthought, it reveals a profound failure of leadership and moral responsibility.

These concerns are not isolated incidents. They reflect a broader pattern of governance that, in my view, has shown far too little concern for the safety, health, dignity, and future of children. No administration should be beyond criticism when the well-being of the next generation is at stake.

The examples below further illustrate these concerns.
